Alice Morey's artworks explore biological and ecological entanglements. Her unprimed canvases are typically smeared, splattered, and stained with pigment mixed with yoghurt, merging probiotics with painting. Drawings of flora and fauna jostle for attention next to moments of abstractions. They trade on a tension between figuration and something more reticent. These works zoom in and out – the pictures contain their own logic, but Morey reminds us that ultimately art – despite its symbols and codes conceived and decoded by humans over thousands of years – remains as unknown as the world it inhabits.
Text: George Vasey Vitamin D3: Today's Best in Contemporary Drawing, Phaidon.
Alice Morey (b. 1986 in London, UK, lives in Berlin) studied at the Camberwell College of Arts London, UK, and the University of Brighton, UK, before completing her Masters at Chelsea College of Arts London, UK, in 2019. Her recent solo exhibitions include, One Thing Left to Try MEWO Kunsthalle Memmingen in 2022 curated by Lorenzo Graf. Her work has been presented at Lehmann + Silva in Porto, Portugal; The Ryder, London, UK; Wells Project London, UK; Tate Britain in collaboration with Chelsea College of Arts, London, UK; Tschechisches Zentrum Berlin; and Haus am Lützowplatz, Berlin, among others.
Morey is the founder and curator of an alternative collaborative art residency Countdown Grabowsee (Globe Gallery), which takes place each summer in an abandoned sanatorium north of Berlin. She often works in collaborations and is part of three active collectives: Portland (UK), Data_Blood (UK) and Puppetmaster (D). In 2019, she was artist-in-residence at Camden Arts Centre before completing a residency at the Academy of Visual Arts in Hong Kong. She has also developed a body of work at the VAADS Academy of Art in Tbilisi, Georgia.
